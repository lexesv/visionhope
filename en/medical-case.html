Medical Case

This page briefly explains my diagnoses and treatment history.

Main diagnoses

  • Pellucid Marginal Degeneration (PMD)
  • Grade I PMD in the right eye
  • Grade IV PMD in the left eye
  • High myopia in both eyes
  • Peripheral vitreochorioretinal / chorioretinal degeneration

Treatment performed

  • UV-X / cross-linking of the right eye
  • Emergency peripheral retinal laser treatment of both eyes in 2008 (records unavailable)
  • Repeat peripheral retinal laser treatment of both eyes in 2021
  • Regular corneal and retinal examinations
  • Unsuccessful scleral-lens adaptation: significant pain, tearing, discomfort and unstable vision

Why the case is difficult

PMD and keratoconus-like conditions cause irregular corneal thinning and deformation. In my case, the left eye is significantly worse, and the options available in Ukraine no longer provide the expected improvement.

Scleral lenses did not become a viable alternative either. After approximately two weeks of adaptation, they still caused significant pain, tearing and severe discomfort, while my vision remained unstable. Long-term wear was therefore not possible.

During consultations, I was told about the risk of further severe damage to the left cornea. If that happens, urgent surgery may be required, and the result cannot be predicted in advance.

Why Israel

After exhausting the options available in Ukraine, I began looking for clinics experienced in complex corneal cases. This is why specialized clinics in Israel are being considered.
