Almost 20 years of fighting to preserve my vision

For almost 20 years, I have lived with a condition that is gradually taking away my ability to see normally. My left eye is severely affected, and the available methods of vision correction have not provided a usable result. I am now seeking treatment at a specialized clinic in Israel and openly publishing my medical records, treatment history and fundraising progress.

Support TreatmentView Documents

Why this website exists

My deteriorating vision affects everyday life, computer work, reading and the ability to manage ordinary tasks independently. Treatment abroad is financially beyond my reach without the support of other people.

This website is not built on exaggeration. It brings together the facts and documents that confirm my journey: from the earliest examinations and cross-linking to emergency retinal laser treatment of both eyes in 2008, repeat treatment in 2021, recent consultations and the search for further care. Records of the 2008 procedure are no longer available.

Key diagnoses

  • Pellucid Marginal Degeneration (PMD)
  • PMD grade I in the right eye
  • PMD grade IV in the left eye
  • High myopia in both eyes
  • Status after cross-linking and retinal laser treatment

Why scleral lenses were not a solution

In 2019, I tried to adapt to scleral lenses for approximately two weeks. They caused significant pain, tearing and persistent discomfort, while my vision remained unstable and did not provide sufficient visual quality.

As a result, I could not use scleral lenses as a practical long-term method of vision correction. This was not a refusal of an available solution, but an unsuccessful attempt that did not produce a usable result.

Outcome of the trial

  • significant pain and tearing;
  • persistent severe discomfort;
  • unstable vision;
  • inability to wear the lenses for extended periods.
