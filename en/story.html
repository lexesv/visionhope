My Journey to Treatment

This is a personal story, but it is based on medical documents, doctors’ reports and confirmed procedures.

The beginning

In 2006, doctors diagnosed me with keratoconus. Later, the diagnosis was clarified as pellucid marginal degeneration of the cornea, or PMD. It is a complex condition in which the cornea becomes thinner and distorted, gradually reducing vision.

Treatment in Ukraine

In 2008, I underwent corneal cross-linking of the right eye. That same year, emergency peripheral retinal laser treatment was performed on both eyes at a public clinic before an exact diagnosis had been established. Medical records of this procedure are no longer available.

Over the following years, I had regular examinations, including Orbscan, Visante OCT, Pentacam, pachymetry and corneal topography.

In 2020–2021, the documents recorded not only corneal disease but also peripheral retinal changes. Because of that, in 2021 I underwent prophylactic peripheral laser coagulation of both retinas.

Scleral lenses

In 2019, I tried scleral lenses as one of the few non-surgical ways to correct an irregular cornea. I attempted to adapt to them for approximately two weeks, but the lenses caused significant pain, tearing and persistent discomfort. Even while wearing them, my vision remained unstable and the visual quality was not sufficient for normal everyday use.

Because of this, I could not continue wearing them. Despite a genuine adaptation attempt, scleral lenses did not become a practical method of vision correction for me.

Recent consultations

In 2026, after new examinations, I consulted specialists again. I was told that Ukrainian clinics can mostly monitor the condition or perform separate procedures if it worsens, but cannot offer a solution that would significantly improve my vision.

The war

Because of the war, my family lost our home and had to start over. Under these circumstances, treatment abroad became financially impossible without the support of other people.

Why I made this website

I created this website to openly show the documents, the timeline and the fundraising progress. I do not ask anyone to trust me blindly — I want people to be able to see the facts.
